💡 What Does Each-Way Mean?

Each-way is two bets in one:

Bet 1: The horse wins the race

Bet 2: The horse finishes 2nd, 3rd or 4th

A £1 each-way bet costs £2 total — £1 on winning, £1 on placing. A £2 each-way costs £4 total.

Even if your horse doesn't win, you still get paid if it places. All UK bookmakers offer this — just tick the each-way box on your betslip.

Each-way — I've never heard of it. What does it mean?
Each-way is two bets in one. Half your stake goes on the horse winning. The other half goes on it finishing in the top places.

So a £1 each-way bet costs £2 total — £1 on winning, £1 on placing.
A £2 each-way costs £4 total.

The big advantage: if your horse doesn't win but finishes close, you still get paid. All UK bookmakers offer this. When you add a horse to your betslip, just tick the "each-way" box before placing your bet.
What happens if only one horse wins?
You still get money back. One winner usually returns your stake with a small profit. Two winners makes a solid profit. Three winners on the same day produces a much bigger return as all three payouts stack together. Signal 75 has had at least one winner or placer in every single betting day tracked so far.

Yes — both. Flat racing is horses running on a flat track. Jumps racing is horses going over hurdles or fences. The app has a separate Jumps tab. One unlock covers everything — flat and jumps horses 2 and 3 are both revealed.
