💡 What Does Each-Way Mean?

Each-way is two bets in one:

Bet 1: The horse wins the race

Bet 2: The horse finishes 2nd, 3rd or 4th

A £1 each-way bet costs £2 total — £1 on winning, £1 on placing. A £2 each-way costs £4 total.

Even if your horse doesn't win, you still get paid if it places. All UK bookmakers offer this — just tick the each-way box on your betslip.

🐎 The Simple Version

What Signal 75 Does

Every morning Signal 75 scans all of today's UK horse races. It scores every horse based on form, tipster opinion, odds value and field size. The three strongest horses become your picks for the day.

You take those picks to your bookmaker — Bet365, Betfair, William Hill, whoever you use — and back them each-way.

Why Three Horses?

Three horses from three different races spreads your risk across the day. If one doesn't go your way, you've still got two others running.

💰 What Each Outcome Means For You

1 winner or placer — you usually get your stake back, sometimes a small profit

2 winners — solid profit on the day

3 winners — all three combine to produce a much bigger return

How a Pick Is Chosen

1

Today's Races Scanned

Every UK race is checked. Races with fewer than 8 horses are skipped — small fields are harder to predict.

2

Every Horse Scored Out of 100

Based on: how many professional tipsters back it, whether the odds represent value, the size of the field, and recent form. Minimum score of 65 to qualify.

3

Strict Filters Applied

Odds must be between evens and 5/1. At least 6 professional tipsters must back the horse. No exceptions.

4

Three Picks Chosen

The best qualifying horse from three different races becomes today's selections. If fewer than three pass — no picks that day. We never force a selection.
